Machu Picchu, Peru

About Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu was built by the Inca Yupanqui people during the mid-15th century for emperor Pachacuti. This historical site sits on top of a granite mountain. The structural integrity of Machu Picchu is made up of stones that fit together seamlessly which is the reason of its great preservation. Because the Spanish never rediscovered this site, it was reclaimed by jungle growth. Thanks to the American explorer, Hiram Bingham in 1911, we are able to admire the great structure today.
